There are many maps of New York City.

They show streets, subways, buildings — where we live.

But they don't show how we live.

In an increasingly digital world, the experiences that define city life risk going unseen — unmapped, forgotten.

Yet the boundary between physical and digital is dissolving. And that creates something new: the possibility to rediscover the city we already live in.

'The city fosters art and is art; the city creates the theater and is the theater.'
— Lewis Mumford, 1937

Every place has a social character. Described by the people who go there.

Places connect across the city — not by proximity, but by the social character they share.

Pull the thread and the whole city connects.

From a bookstore in the Village to a nightclub in the Bronx — the same city, the same social fabric.
